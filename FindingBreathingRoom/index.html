🔍 Understanding What You're Experiencing

Major Depressive Disorder

What it is: A mood disorder characterized by persistent low mood, low energy, poor sleep, decreased appetite, and difficulty functioning

In your case: Moderate in severity

Important to know: Depression and anxiety can be responses to overwhelming circumstances, not just "chemical imbalances"

Adjustment Disorder

What it is: Emotional or behavioral symptoms (like depression or anxiety) that arise in response to a significant life stressor

For you: The stressors are work, school, and marital changes

Anxiety

What it involves: Worry and physical symptoms (tension, restlessness)

Can be mental: "In your head" (worry, rumination)

Can be physical: "In your body" (tension, restlessness)

Important Clarifications

Depression and anxiety are not personal failings

They are responses to overwhelming circumstances

"Taking leave or using services is not 'failing' — it's a healthy response to overload."

🌬️ Framework #1: The Overwhelm-Relief Model

"You're probably going through with just sheer momentum."

The Five-Step Process

1 Identify All Stressors

What to do: List out all major demands

For you: Work, school, marriage, home

Why this matters: Identifying stressors clarifies what's draining you

2 Acknowledge Limits

What to recognize: No one can do everything at once without consequences

Why this matters: Acknowledging limits challenges the "I must do it all" belief

3 Seek Breathing Room

What to do: Find areas where relief is possible

Examples:

  • Workplace accommodations
  • Delegating tasks
  • Outsourcing household responsibilities

Why this matters: Seeking relief prevents burnout and allows for recovery

4 Communicate Needs

What to do: Share the reality of your situation with your partner and others

"If your spouse wants you to talk to them, I think that you should talk to them about bills. So that they're in the know. Because you don't have it all. And you can't be expected to have it all."

Why this matters: Communicating needs builds understanding and support

5 Prioritize Recovery

What to do: Make space for rest, sleep, and self-care

Why this matters: Prioritizing recovery is essential for mental and physical health

The Logic Behind This Framework:

When you're overwhelmed, continuing to push through without relief leads to collapse.

Creating space — by stepping back from some responsibilities — allows for healing and prevents further decline.

📊 Before vs. After

Before: You try to do everything yourself, leading to exhaustion and resentment

After: You communicate your limits, seek help, and share responsibilities, leading to more balance and less stress

🏠 Framework #2: The Two-Person Household Principle

"A relationship is two people. Two people to accommodate. Two people have to live together. In the best way possible."

The Five-Step Process

1 Inventory Responsibilities

What to do: List all household and financial tasks

Why this matters: Inventorying tasks prevents invisible labor

2 Share Information

What to do: Make sure both partners know what's being done and what's needed

"Ignorance is bliss. And so, you know, it helps to show, it helps to say, like, hey. Here's how much we're paying. Right? Just so you can at least know."

Tools to use: Spreadsheets, bills, lists

Why this matters: Sharing information avoids resentment and "ignorance is bliss" dynamics

3 Negotiate Fairness

What to do: Discuss what feels equitable, not just traditional roles

Why this matters: Negotiating fairness ensures both partners feel valued

4 Adjust Standards

What to do: Agree on what "good enough" looks like for your home and life

Why this matters: Adjusting standards reduces perfectionism and stress

5 Use Outside Help

What to consider: Services like cleaning, laundry to reduce the load

"Nobody wants to get clothes out of the hamper and they're all balled up. You gotta put more time in the dryer. But well, I'm not kidding."

Why this matters: Outside help frees up time and energy

The Logic Behind This Framework:

A relationship is a partnership. When one person carries too much, both suffer.

Transparency and teamwork create a more sustainable, supportive environment.

How This Applies to You

You are expected to manage finances and household tasks, but your spouse may not be fully aware of the burden. By sharing information (spreadsheets, bills), you can foster more understanding and potentially renegotiate responsibilities.

💊 Medication Plan

Adderall 10mg - STIMULANT (BEING CONSIDERED)

Important to understand:

Adderall is a stimulant medication most commonly prescribed for Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). In your case, you do not have ADHD, but the medication is being considered to address the most immediate needs of low energy, poor focus, and fatigue associated with depression and overwhelming stress.

How to take:

  • Start with 10mg in the morning to get used to the medication and monitor for side effects
  • If tolerated, a second 10mg dose can be taken in the early afternoon
  • Do not take Adderall after 2pm as it can interfere with sleep

Expected benefits:

  • Increased alertness and energy
  • Improved concentration and ability to function during the day

Side effects:

  • Initial side effects may include feeling "hyperactive," jitteriness, decreased appetite, or trouble sleeping
  • These effects often lessen as your body adjusts

Safety warnings:

  • Use only as prescribed
  • Monitor for side effects, especially insomnia or increased anxiety
  • Not intended as a long-term solution, but as a short-term aid while other supports are put in place

Rationale: The primary issue is exhaustion and inability to function due to depression and stress. Adderall, though not indicated for depression, can provide short-term relief of fatigue and poor focus, helping you regain momentum while addressing underlying issues.

"I'll call you later with more information about meds or something that or maybe tomorrow. But we'll talk more about that."

Over-the-Counter (OTC) Sleep Aid

Recommendation: Try an OTC sleep aid (such as melatonin or diphenhydramine) to help fall asleep, especially if stimulant medication causes difficulty sleeping

How to take: Use as directed on the package, before bedtime

Expected benefits: Improved ability to fall asleep and stay asleep

Side effects:

  • Possible morning grogginess or excessive tiredness
  • If you experience excessive tiredness, this could be a side effect of the sleep aid, the stimulant, or a phenomenon called "rebound sleep" — which occurs after long periods of inadequate sleep as your body tries to catch up

Safety warnings:

  • Do not combine with alcohol or other sedatives
  • Use only as needed and monitor for next-day drowsiness

Rationale: Poor sleep is worsening mood and energy. Improving sleep is a priority for overall recovery, and sleep aids may help break the cycle of insomnia and exhaustion.
