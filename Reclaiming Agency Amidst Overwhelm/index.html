🫕 Framework #1: The Pressure Cooker Model

"You're sitting in a pressure cooker. The pressure just keeps building up."

The Three-Step Process

1 Recognize Accumulating Stress ("Pressure") from Unresolved Issues

What happens: Each unaddressed issue adds pressure

In your case: Family conflicts, legal battles, health crisis, financial stress — all building simultaneously

The metaphor: Like meat in a pressure cooker — eventually it gets "overcooked," falling off the bones

Your body is showing this: Hypertension, aneurysm

2 Identify Sources of Pressure

Your sources:

  • Family: Ex-partners manipulating, mother with broken trust
  • Legal: Multiple lawsuits, custody battles
  • Health: Aneurysm diagnosis, activity restrictions, medication concerns
  • Financial: Unemployment, $1,600/month child support, legal expenses

3 Release Pressure by Expressing Feelings and Asserting Boundaries

How to release:

  • Express feelings (even in writing)
  • Assert boundaries (even if uncomfortable)
  • Take action (legal steps, direct communication)

The therapist's guidance: "Let some of the steam out, little by little. Let people know."

⚠️ What Happens When Pressure Isn't Released

Unreleased pressure leads to emotional and physical breakdown:

  • Hypertension
  • Aneurysm
  • Emotional explosions
  • Health deterioration

The logic: When you don't address issues, the pressure builds. Eventually, it finds a way out — often through your health or a blow-up.

💪 Framework #2: Agency and Assertiveness

"You have some agency. You have a choice."

The Three-Step Process

1 Acknowledge Your Agency

What agency means: The ability to choose and act

The truth: You have more power than you feel

What's happened: You've been conditioned to defer to others

The therapist's insight: "They're banking on the fact that you won't fight back — because you don't, typically."

2 Identify Where You've Been Passive or Accommodating

Your pattern:

  • Allowing ex-partners to overstep boundaries
  • Not enforcing boundaries with your mother
  • Being passive at work
  • Avoiding legal action even when necessary

The result: Others have been able to "steamroll" you

The metaphor: "You've been bent over for a minute now. They just keep following through."

3 Take Concrete Action, Even If Uncomfortable

What action looks like:

  • Legal steps (going to court, contacting lawyer)
  • Direct communication (stating boundaries clearly)
  • Following through on necessary tasks (job applications, health appointments)

The therapist's reminder: "The only thing you'll be starting is your fight in that."

Why discomfort is necessary: Your old identity as "peacekeeper" must shift for things to change

✓ Why This Framework Matters

Others rely on your inaction

Reclaiming agency disrupts unhealthy patterns

When you act, you change the game


The logic: They're banking on you not fighting back because you usually don't. When you act, you change the game.
