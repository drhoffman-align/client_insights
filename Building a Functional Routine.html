Therapeutic Insights

Alignment Psychiatry

The Situation

Ashley struggles with maintaining a consistent routine due to fluctuating energy levels, a demanding 7am-5:30pm work schedule, and requiring 9-11 hours of sleep nightly. She has ADHD managed with stimulant medication and finds it difficult to balance chores, meal prep, and self-care, often accomplishing only one major task per day. Weekends become catch-up periods rather than restful breaks, and she experiences difficulty transitioning between work and home life.

Core Problem

"Ashley's current routines don't match her energy and attention patterns, causing inefficiency, overwhelm, and inconsistent self-care."

She over-identifies with sleep as part of her personality, which reinforces excessive sleep and avoidance of morning productivity, while attempting too much at once leads to burnout.

Clinical Concepts

Time-Blocking

Divides your day into set periods for specific activities to reduce overwhelm.

Sleep Hygiene

Creates routines promoting consistent, restorative sleep.

Habit Formation

Requires three months of consistent practice to establish new patterns.

Reframing

Changes how you think about something to shift perspective.

Decision Fatigue

Mental exhaustion from too many choices throughout the day.

Medication Titration

Adjusts medication types and timing to match energy needs.

Therapeutic Frameworks

Time-Blocking and Prioritization Framework

  1. Identify fixed commitments and anchor points in your schedule
  2. Reserve time blocks for psychological needs and self-care
  3. Prioritize one major task daily to prevent overwhelm
  4. Schedule non-negotiable sleep blocks to protect rest
  5. Use visual cues and reminders to reinforce your routine

Sleep Reframing and Habit Formation

  1. Set a specific start date for your new routine
  2. Mentally rehearse the new routine in advance for 1-2 weeks
  3. Focus on hours of sleep rather than clock time
  4. Reframe sleep as a functional tool, not part of your identity
  5. Remove all waking barriers by preparing the night before
  6. Commit to three months of consistent practice for lasting change

Meal Prep Efficiency

  1. Create a list of 5-10 easy, go-to meals
  2. Use batch cooking and ready-made options without guilt
  3. Order groceries online for pickup to save time and energy
  4. Accept that meals don't need to be elaborate to be nourishing

Practical Instructions

Medication Plan: Long-acting Ritalin 40mg upon waking provides sustained workday focus. Short-acting methylphenidate 20mg taken 4-6 hours later as needed for additional focus. Take long-acting immediately upon waking (pre-set by bedside). Monitor for increased heart rate, anxiety, and appetite changes. Adjust timing if overlap causes discomfort.

Daily Structure: Complete 20 minutes of daily chores before relaxing after work. This creates a transition buffer between work and home life while preventing weekend catch-up overwhelm. Use time-blocking to structure both evenings and mornings, maintaining a consistent seven-day routine including weekends.

"Reframe sleep as an energy tool, not your identity. Focus on sleep hours, not clock time."

Homework

  • Set specific date to start new sleep/wake routine
  • Mentally rehearse new routine nightly for 1-2 weeks
  • Create/re-post time cards around home as activity cues
  • Make list of 5-10 go-to meals
  • Begin ordering groceries online and batch prepping meals
  • Complete 20 minutes of daily chores before relaxing after work

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize one major task daily to prevent overwhelm
  • Use time-blocking to structure evenings and mornings
  • Reframe sleep as energy tool, not identity
  • Set specific start date—don't change everything at once
  • Remove all barriers to medication and waking
  • Focus on sleep hours, not clock time
  • Batch-cook meals and use ready-made options
  • Complete 20 minutes of daily chores before relaxing
  • Maintain consistent seven-day routine including weekends
  • Commit to three months for habit formation
  • Use visual cues to reinforce schedule
  • Adjust medication timing to match energy needs
Bottom Line: Your routines need to match your energy patterns, not fight against them. By prioritizing one task daily, reframing sleep as a tool rather than an identity, and removing barriers to success, you can create sustainable habits that support your wellbeing. Remember: consistency over three months creates lasting change, and progress matters more than perfection.
