Therapeutic Insights

Alignment Psychiatry

The Situation

You are experiencing severe burnout, emotional exhaustion, and anxiety stemming from chronic over-functioning in relationships and work. You struggle with perfectionism, hypervigilance, and fear of abandonment rooted in childhood trauma. Sleep disturbances, migraines, and increased emotionality have worsened. You're navigating a relationship with an avoidant partner while managing your own need for control and immediate resolution.

"Burnout signals that current coping strategies are unsustainable. Your worth isn't defined by productivity or others' approval."

Core Problem

You over-function by taking excessive responsibility for others' emotions and outcomes, driven by perfectionism and fear of abandonment. This pattern, once adaptive, now causes chronic anxiety, emotional depletion, and inability to access joy.

Clinical Concepts

Burnout
Exhaustion from prolonged stress and over-responsibility.
Perfectionism
Belief that impossibly high standards equal worthiness.
Hypervigilance
Constant alertness for danger from trauma.
Fear of Abandonment
Deep worry others will leave, causing relationship over-functioning.
Adaptability
Ability to adjust to circumstances and remain flexible.
"Adaptability, not control, reduces stress and restores joy. Self-compassion means treating yourself as kindly as you treat others."

Therapeutic Frameworks

Adaptability Over Control
1
Recognize limits of control
2
Identify areas needing flexibility
3
Practice allowing unexpected outcomes
4
Integrate past lessons as resources not rigid templates
Self-Compassion and Externalization
1
View yourself as you would a valued friend
2
Separate worth from outcomes
3
Embrace imperfection as human
Problem-Solving for Self
1
Apply problem-solving skills to your own needs
2
Clarify goals through lists and diagrams
3
Test tangible actions to clarify feelings
Key Insight: Distinguish between your burdens and others' responsibilities. Direct problem-solving energy toward your own needs. Rest and downtime are necessary, not optional.

Practical Instructions

Medication Management:

Clonazepam
Increase to 2mg nightly for sleep and anxiety. Monitor grogginess and dependence risk. Avoid alcohol and sedatives.
Escitalopram
Continue 20mg daily. Monitor effectiveness.
Cannabis
Use sparingly; rely primarily on prescribed medication.

Homework

Complete the following before your next session in a quiet space (30-60 minutes):

  • List what brings joy versus what drains energy
  • Diagram current life goals—identify self-directed versus reactive ones
  • Journal about true desires in relationships (marriage, children, partnership)
  • Optional: Visit bridal shop to explore emotional response to wedding dress
"Tangible experiments clarify true desires better than rumination. Trust yourself and community rather than relying solely on rigid self-sufficiency."

Key Takeaways

  • Burnout signals current coping strategies are unsustainable
  • Adaptability, not control, reduces stress and restores joy
  • Your worth isn't defined by productivity or others' approval
  • Distinguish between your burdens and others' responsibilities
  • Direct problem-solving energy toward your own needs
  • Self-compassion means treating yourself as kindly as you treat others
  • Rest and downtime are necessary, not optional
  • Tangible experiments clarify true desires better than rumination
  • Take medication as prescribed; communicate about effectiveness
  • Trust yourself and community rather than relying solely on rigid self-sufficiency
Bottom Line: You have carried the weight of the world with remarkable strength, but sustainable healing requires releasing what was never yours to carry. By embracing adaptability over control and extending to yourself the same compassion you offer others, you create space for joy, rest, and authentic connection. Your worth is inherent—not earned through exhaustion.
