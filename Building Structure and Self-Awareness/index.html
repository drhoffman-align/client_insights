💊 Framework #1: Medication Optimization

"As long as you take them as prescribed, you'll be fine."

The Four-Step Approach

1 Understand the Difference Between Your Two Medications

Vyvanse (long-acting):

  • Subtle, sustained effect
  • Provides all-day coverage
  • Steady focus and motivation

Adderall (short-acting):

  • Stronger, immediate boost
  • Quick onset
  • More noticeable effect

2 Use Both Medications Together in the Morning

Current approach: Take Vyvanse and Adderall together in the morning as prescribed

Purpose: To achieve sufficient stimulation and motivation from the start of your day

3 Consider Adjusting for Better All-Day Coverage

Potential optimization: Increase Vyvanse for all-day coverage

Then: Reserve Adderall for "as needed" situations

The therapist's guidance: "You can take the Adderall as needed if you need it, like, in the afternoon or something for, like, PRN or as needed."

Benefit: Prevents overuse, reduces dependency risk

4 Take Medication Breaks to Prevent Tolerance

Strategy: Take medication regularly during workweek, with breaks on weekends

The therapist's explanation: "Most people take a day or two over the weekend to keep their brain online and to keep the medication sort of working."

Why this matters: Prevents tolerance and dependency

🧠 Understanding Psychological vs. Physiological Dependency

Psychological Dependency

What it is: Feeling like you "need" medication to function, even if not physically addicted

Example: People who take anxiety meds for flying may panic if they forget their meds — not because of the flight, but because they lack the medication

The concern: Relying on medication for all productivity rather than building internal coping skills

Physiological Dependency

What it is: Body adapting to medication, requiring higher doses for same effect

The mechanism (Tolerance): "The brain is supposed to do the job, but your brain is like, 'Oh, well, something else is doing that. I can stop.' That's how people get used to a medication and then they need higher doses."

How it develops: Brain can "taper back" its own production of neurotransmitters if medication used constantly

Addiction vs. Prescribed Use

The therapist's reassurance: "Addiction happens when you take it and you kinda get high. But if you're taking it as prescribed, you'll be fine."

"I'm not worried about addiction at all. It is very hard to get addicted on these medications mostly because they're scheduled, and you can only get a certain amount."

⚠️ What to Monitor

  • Don't take medication outside of prescribed needs
  • Be mindful of relying on medication for all productivity
  • Monitor for signs of dependency or overuse
  • Communicate with therapist if you feel need to increase doses

📅 Framework #2: Blocked Time Schedule

"The first thing that I think that you'll have to establish is a blocked time schedule."

The Four-Step Process

1 Identify Broad "Areas of Life"

Examples:

  • Personal development
  • Work
  • Family
  • Fitness
  • Self-care

2 Allocate Daily Time Blocks to Each Area

The approach: Ensure regular attention without rigid routines

Flexibility: Don't have to do same thing every day, but must do something in that area every day

3 Within "Work," Create Sub-Blocks for Specific Tasks

Examples of work sub-blocks:

  • Business development
  • Operations
  • Email management
  • Vendor communications
  • Staff management

Benefit: Instead of reacting to every email as it comes, you'll have set times to process emails, freeing up mental space for deeper work

4 Use the Schedule to Streamline Thinking

Purpose: Reduce overwhelm and maintain balance

Structure without rigidity: Provides framework while allowing daily variation

✓ Why Block Scheduling Works for ADHD

"Block scheduling is really good for ADHD people because it puts you in blocks and you don't have to do the same thing every day, but you have to do something in that area every day."


Provides structure without monotony

Ensures all important areas receive attention

Reduces decision fatigue

Prevents overwhelm from multiple responsibilities

💝 Framework #3: Feelings Wheel & Values Clarification

"Your mission and values and purpose — those should come from feelings."

The Three-Step Process

1 List All Stakeholders

Who interacts with your business:

  • Clients
  • Clients' clients
  • Employees

2 Define Desired Feelings for Each Stakeholder

What to do: For each stakeholder group, define the feelings you want them to experience when interacting with your business

Tool: Use the Feelings Wheel as a guide

Organize by: Interaction type (front desk, billing, service delivery, etc.)

3 Use Feelings to Inform Mission, Values, and Brand

The insight: These emotional outcomes should guide your company's mission, values, and brand identity

Why this matters: Creates more authentic, impactful business and guides decision-making

How to Develop Your Mission Statement:

Step 1: Think of your company as a person, not a business

What kind of character would your company have? What would it stand for?

Step 2: Determine what you would like to solve

Not just now, but in the long run too. What problem exists that you want to address? What impact do you want to make over time?

Step 3: Brainstorm by writing down everything that comes to mind

Don't filter — just capture all ideas, goals, aspirations, and problems you want to solve

Step 4: Use AI tools to synthesize your ideas

After brainstorming, use tools like ChatGPT or Claude AI to help you generate 1-2 sentences that encompass everything you want your business to achieve

How to Develop Your Company Values:

What values are: Like promises that you want your company to uphold. The fundamental beliefs and guiding principles that define your organization's culture, shape decision-making, and dictate how employees act and interact.

Step 1: Brainstorm first

Write down words or concepts that you want to be your company's guiding principles. What matters most? What standards do you want to maintain? What principles are non-negotiable?

Step 2: Use AI tools to refine into value statements

Use tools like ChatGPT or Claude AI to help you generate no more than 10 value statements that encompass every value you wrote down

The result: Clear, concise values that guide every decision and interaction in your business

✓ Why This Approach Matters

Clarifying desired emotional outcomes creates more authentic, impactful business

Guides decision-making and branding

Crucial for business growth and satisfaction

Creates stronger, more cohesive business identity

💊 Medication Plan

Vyvanse - LONG-ACTING STIMULANT

Current dose: Small (may increase to 20mg as needed)

Timing: Take in morning

How to take: With or without food

Purpose: Provides steady, long-acting focus and motivation for ADHD

Expected benefits: All-day coverage for focus, motivation, and productivity

Side effects to monitor: Sleep disruption, appetite changes, jitteriness

Safety warnings: Do not exceed prescribed dose. Take breaks on weekends to prevent tolerance.

Rationale: Offers subtle, sustained effect. Increasing dose may provide all-day support, reducing need for additional stimulants.

Adderall - SHORT-ACTING STIMULANT

Dosage: 5mg, short-acting

Timing: Take in morning with Vyvanse or as needed in afternoon

How to take: As prescribed, not more than directed

Purpose: Provides stronger, immediate boost in motivation and energy

Expected benefits: Quick onset of focus and energy when needed

Side effects to monitor: Sleep issues if taken late, increased heart rate, anxiety

Safety warnings: Use only as needed; avoid taking late in the day

Rationale: Reserved for times when extra focus is required, preventing overuse and dependency

General Medication Guidance

  • Take medications as prescribed
  • Use weekends as "medication holidays" to prevent tolerance
  • Monitor for signs of dependency or overuse
  • Communicate with therapist if you feel need to increase doses or experience side effects
  • Monitor for sleep disruption and adjust timing if needed
