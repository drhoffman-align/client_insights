Therapeutic Insights

Alignment Psychiatry - Session Summary

The Situation

You are navigating a painful marital separation marked by high anxiety, disrupted sleep, and feelings of abandonment. You have provided materially and emotionally for your wife but face accusations, deflection, and lack of reciprocal care. You are managing anxiety and ADHD medications while preparing for couples therapy tonight.

Core Problem

You struggle to set boundaries and protect yourself emotionally due to deep fear of abandonment and a pattern of prioritizing your partner's needs over your own. This self-sacrifice leaves you vulnerable to manipulation and prevents you from asserting your truth and worth.

"Your worth isn't defined by your wife's inability to recognize it."

Clinical Concepts

Gaslighting
Manipulation causing you to doubt your own reality or memories
Boundaries
Limits protecting your emotional well-being
Deflection
Shifting blame away from oneself to avoid accountability
Self-Assertion
Communicating needs and truth directly and confidently

Therapeutic Frameworks

Evidence-Based Self-Assertion
1
Gather concrete evidence of your contributions - specific actions, dates, and examples of what you've provided
2
Articulate your truth with specific examples rather than generalizations
3
Anticipate deflection by requesting evidence in return when faced with vague accusations
4
Maintain composure - self-assertion without anger demonstrates true confidence
5
Set boundaries based on your personal values, not based on her reactions
"Start Low, Go Slow" Medication Adjustment
1
Begin with low dose increases to assess tolerance
2
Monitor effects carefully over several days
3
Gradually increase as tolerated to find optimal balance
4
Track symptoms and side effects for provider communication
"Use concrete evidence to reclaim your narrative and counter manipulation."

Practical Instructions

For Tonight's Couples Therapy

  • Write a clear statement listing your specific contributions and actions
  • Contrast what you provided versus what you received
  • State clear boundaries and expectations
  • Practice calm delivery aloud before the session
  • Enter with confidence, not self-doubt
  • When faced with vague accusations, ask for specifics

Medication Adjustments

Clonazepam: Increase to 1mg (two 0.5mg pills) at bedtime for anxiety and sleep support.

Adderall: Increase to one-and-a-half pills. Monitor effects before considering two pills.

Clinical Rationale: Your high tolerance and persistent symptoms require gradual dose increases to find optimal balance between symptom relief and side effects while ensuring safety.

Safety Warnings

  • Avoid alcohol with clonazepam
  • Don't drive if drowsy
  • Monitor for dependence risk with benzodiazepines
  • Don't exceed prescribed Adderall dose
  • Watch for heart symptoms (rapid heartbeat, chest pain)

Side Effects to Monitor

Clonazepam: Drowsiness, next-day grogginess, tolerance development

Adderall: Insomnia, jitteriness, appetite suppression, mood changes
