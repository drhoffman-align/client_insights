Therapeutic Insights

Alignment Psychiatry

The Situation

You are a Black woman ophthalmologist navigating overwhelming stress from multiple sources simultaneously. Workplace scrutiny amplified by racial and gender bias, military conflicts with a new commander that jeopardized your civilian job, co-parenting legal challenges, multiple pending medical referrals, and relationship strain are all converging at once. You feel disconnected from your nursing staff who bypass you to report directly to the CMO, undermining your leadership authority. With limited OR time of only 1-2 days monthly, you have restricted contact with your team, leaving you feeling excluded and reactive rather than in control of your professional environment.

"Not all problems can be solved simultaneously; triage and focus on controllable factors."

Core Problem

You are experiencing chronic overwhelm from simultaneous, unrelenting stressors that are compounded by systemic bias and a lack of trust with your team. There is a painful mismatch between your expertise and others' perceptions of you, leading to defensiveness, disengagement, and a loss of agency in both your professional relationships and personal well-being. This pattern keeps you in a reactive stance rather than allowing you to lead from a place of confidence and intentionality.

Clinical Concepts

Personal Branding

Personal branding is the practice of deliberately shaping how others perceive your professional identity. For you, this means proactively defining and communicating your values, expertise, and leadership approach to counteract bias and assert your authority. Rather than allowing others' assumptions to define you, you take control of the narrative.

Systemic Bias

Systemic bias refers to unfair treatment patterns embedded in organizational structures based on race, gender, or other identities. Recognizing these patterns helps you understand that challenges you face are not personal failures but reflections of broader inequities that require strategic responses.

Intentionality

Intentionality means acting with clear purpose rather than simply reacting to circumstances. It involves pausing to consider your goals and values before responding, allowing you to maintain agency even in difficult situations.

Triage

Triage is the practice of prioritizing urgent issues when you are overwhelmed by multiple demands. It acknowledges that not everything can be addressed at once and helps you focus your limited energy on what matters most right now.

Trust Equation

Trust is built through three essential components: shared knowledge, shared experiences, and consistent time together. Understanding this equation helps you see that trust deficits with your team are not about your worth but about insufficient opportunities for connection.

Therapeutic Frameworks

Personal Branding and Leadership Identity

This framework helps you intentionally shape how others perceive your leadership, counteracting bias and establishing your authority.

1
Define Your Brand: Clarify your core values, strengths, and vision as a leader. What do you want to be known for? What principles guide your decisions?
2
Communicate Intentionally: Actively share your brand through your words and actions. Narrate your thought process, explain your methodical approach, and make your expertise visible.
3
Model Desired Culture: Embody the respect, excellence, and collaboration you want to see. Your behavior sets the tone for your team.
4
Diagnose Problems: When misunderstandings occur, identify whether they stem from knowledge gaps, limited shared experiences, or insufficient time together.
5
Iterate and Adjust: Continuously refine your approach based on feedback and outcomes. Leadership is an evolving practice.
"Methodical pausing is a strategic strength—narrate your process to prevent misinterpretation."

Whack-a-Mole Stress Model

This framework acknowledges the reality of multiple simultaneous stressors and provides a strategy for managing them without becoming paralyzed.

1
Recognize the Pattern: Acknowledge that you are dealing with multiple urgent issues at once. This is not a personal failing but a challenging circumstance.
2
Prioritize and Triage: Identify which issues require immediate attention and which can wait. Focus your energy strategically rather than trying to address everything simultaneously.
3
Seek Points of Ease: Intentionally create moments of joy, relaxation, and restoration to sustain your resilience. These are not luxuries but necessities for survival.

Intentional Change Process

This framework provides a structured approach to regaining control and agency when you feel overwhelmed and reactive.

1
Insight/Diagnose: Reflect on your current situation. What patterns do you notice? What is within your control?
2
Analyze: Examine the root causes of challenges. Are they related to knowledge gaps, limited contact, or systemic barriers?
3
Plan: Develop specific, actionable steps to address identified issues. Focus on small, consistent actions rather than overwhelming changes.
4
Test/Act: Implement your plan. Try new approaches and observe the results without judgment.
5
Reflect/Diagnose Again: Review what worked and what didn't. Use these insights to refine your approach and begin the cycle again.
"Daily reflection using the intentional change process regains your sense of control."

Practical Instructions

Building Trust with Your Team

Trust requires three elements: knowledge, experience, and time. With limited OR days, you must maximize every interaction. Use each OR session as an opportunity for teaching, relationship-building, and open communication. Proactively educate your staff about your approach, your reasoning, and your expectations. Share your expertise generously. Create additional touchpoints outside the OR when possible—brief check-ins, team meetings, or informal conversations that build familiarity and connection.

Communicating Your Leadership Brand

Develop a clear, concise statement of your leadership values and vision. When you pause to think during procedures, narrate your thought process: "I'm taking a moment to consider the best approach here because precision matters." When staff bypass you, address it directly but compassionately: "I notice you went to the CMO with that concern. I want you to feel comfortable coming to me first so we can work together effectively." Consistency in these communications gradually reshapes perceptions.

Managing Overwhelm

When multiple crises compete for your attention, pause and triage. Ask yourself: What requires immediate action? What can wait? What is outside my control? Focus your energy on controllable factors and accept that some issues will remain unresolved temporarily. This is strategic prioritization, not failure. Schedule at least one enjoyable or relaxing activity each week as a non-negotiable commitment to your well-being.

Daily Reflection Practice

Dedicate one hour each morning to personal development reflection using the intentional change process. This practice helps you move from reactive to proactive, restoring your sense of agency. Journal your insights, track patterns, and celebrate small victories. This is not indulgent; it is essential maintenance for your mental health and professional effectiveness.

"Stay engaged and communicative rather than withdrawing in protest. Small, consistent actions shift team dynamics over time."

Homework

  • Write a one-page leadership brand statement outlining your values, strengths, and vision as a physician leader
  • Draft a script addressing one recurring workplace misunderstanding, focusing on clear, compassionate communication
  • Schedule one enjoyable or relaxing activity each week for the next month and protect this time
  • Spend one hour each morning for personal development reflection using the intentional change process
  • Identify and schedule at least one additional interaction with nursing staff before your next session
  • Journal your insights, challenges, and progress, and report outcomes at your next appointment

Medication Plan

Continue taking your long-acting stimulant 30mg at 6:30 AM daily to support focus and functioning. You may open the capsule and take half if needed to adjust the dose. Monitor carefully for insomnia if taken late in the day and watch for physiological stimulation. Track the timing of your dose, sleep quality, and effects daily so we can assess whether adjustments are needed.
