🔍 Understanding Key Concepts

Beliefs

Definition: Subjective attitudes or mental convictions that something is true, exists, or often held regardless of proof

In your life: Beliefs about success, worth, and expectations shape your self-talk and motivation

"Beliefs are subjective attitudes or mental convictions that something is true, exists, or often held regardless of proof."

Expectations

What they are: Wishes or predictions about how things "should" go, often inherited from others or past experiences

When unexamined: Can lead to chronic dissatisfaction

"Expectations are wishes at the end of the day."

Insight

What it is: The ability to reflect on one's own thoughts, feelings, and behaviors

How it helps: Developing insight helps you make intentional choices rather than acting on autopilot

"Most people are just living. And have no insight."

Anxiety

What it is: A feeling of unease or worry, often about things outside your control

For you: Anxiety sometimes drives you to keep pushing without pausing to reflect

Time Blocking

What it is: A productivity strategy where specific periods are set aside for particular tasks or areas of life

Benefits: Increases focus and reduces overwhelm

🎛️ Framework #1: Control vs. Lack of Control

"The only thing that we do have control over is our actions. Or what we choose."

The Three-Step Process

1 Identify What Is and Isn't in Your Control

In your control: Actions and decisions

Not in your control: Thoughts, body functions, external events

2 Focus Energy on Making the Best Choices

What to do: With the information you have

3 Accept That Most Things Are Outside Your Control

Important truth: And that's normal

Why this framework matters:

Reduces anxiety and helps direct effort toward what's actually changeable — rather than ruminating on the uncontrollable

For you: You often feel anxious about outcomes (e.g., job search), but many factors are outside your control. By focusing on your actions — like applying for jobs and completing schoolwork — you can reclaim agency and reduce frustration.

You only truly control your actions and decisions — not your thoughts or external events

Anxiety often comes from trying to control the uncontrollable

💭 Framework #2: Expectations and Beliefs

"Beliefs are subjective attitudes or mental convictions that something is true, exists, or often held regardless of proof."

The Four-Step Process

1 Examine Where Your Expectations Come From

Possible sources: Others, past experiences, wishes, beliefs

2 Recognize That Beliefs Are Subjective Attitudes

Important truth: Not objective truths

3 Evaluate Expectations Against Reality

What to consider: Available resources

4 Adapt Expectations as You Gather New Information

Stay flexible: Adjust as you learn

Why this framework matters:

Unchecked expectations can fuel disappointment and anxiety — understanding their origins allows for more realistic self-assessment and goal-setting

For you: Your expectations about how quickly you "should" find a job or feel better are shaped by family, society, and past experiences. Recognizing these as beliefs — not facts — helps you set more compassionate, realistic goals.

Most expectations are inherited or based on beliefs, not facts

Beliefs are subjective and can be challenged or changed

"You rarely stop to pause and say, is this actually for me?"

The Three-Step Process

1 Regularly Pause to Reflect on Your Path

Ask yourself: "Is this for me?"

2 Use Insight to Adjust Course

What to do: Rather than pushing through discomfort blindly

3 Recognize That Most People Don't Do This Until a Crisis

Important truth: Starting now is a strength

"Most people are just living. And have no insight."

Why this framework matters:

Prevents burnout and midlife crisis by fostering self-awareness and intentional living

For you: Rather than pushing through anxiety or discomfort, you are encouraged to pause and reflect on whether your current path aligns with your values and interests, preventing future crises.

Regular self-reflection prevents burnout and midlife crisis

Pausing to ask, "Is this for me?" is a powerful tool for self-direction

Insight is rare but crucial — most people don't develop it until much later

Society's pressure to "just keep pushing" can lead to mental health issues

💊 Medication Plan

Adderall 10mg - FOCUS AND ATTENTION

Dosage/Timing: Once daily in the morning

Purpose: Treats focus and attention difficulties (likely ADHD)

How to take: Take in the morning after waking and before starting daily activities

Expected benefits: Improved focus, energy, and ability to complete tasks

Side effects to monitor: Dry mouth (should be present if medication is effective), insomnia, appetite suppression, jitteriness

Safety warnings: Do not exceed prescribed dose; monitor for increased heart rate, blood pressure, or mood changes

Rationale: Current dose may be subtherapeutic if dry mouth is absent; may increase to 1.5 tablets in the morning if needed, as discussed

"If you aren't as if you don't have dry mouth, then I'm not convinced it's working."

Chantix (Varenicline) - SMOKING CESSATION

Purpose: Smoking cessation

How to take: As prescribed (dose not specified)

Expected benefits: Reduced cravings and withdrawal symptoms

Side effects to monitor: Nausea, vivid dreams, mood changes

Safety warnings: Report any mood changes or suicidal thoughts

Rationale: Supports quitting smoking, especially when nicotine patches alone are insufficient

Nicotine Patch (21mg) - SMOKING CESSATION

Purpose: Smoking cessation

How to take: Apply patch daily as directed

Expected benefits: Reduces withdrawal symptoms and cravings

Side effects to monitor: Skin irritation, sleep disturbances

Safety warnings: Do not smoke while using patch; monitor for signs of overdose

Rationale: Provides steady nicotine to ease transition off cigarettes

Wellbutrin (Bupropion) - MOOD STABILIZATION

Purpose: Mood stabilization, smoking cessation support

How to take: As prescribed (dose not specified)

Expected benefits: Improved mood, reduced cravings

Side effects to monitor: Insomnia, dry mouth, anxiety

Safety warnings: Do not exceed dose; report any mood changes

Rationale: Supports mood and smoking cessation

Atorvastatin - CHOLESTEROL MANAGEMENT

Purpose: Cholesterol management

How to take: As prescribed

Expected benefits: Lower cholesterol, reduced cardiovascular risk

Side effects to monitor: Muscle pain, liver function changes

Safety warnings: Report unexplained muscle pain or weakness

Rationale: Ongoing management of cholesterol
