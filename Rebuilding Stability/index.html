🔍 Understanding Key Concepts

Bipolar I Disorder

What it is: A mood disorder characterized by episodes of mania and depression

Mania can include: Decreased need for sleep, increased energy, risky behavior

Depression involves: Low mood and motivation

"Usually, after a manic episode, you don't really fully recover till like six months to a year."

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder)

What it is: A neurodevelopmental disorder marked by inattention, impulsivity, and difficulty with organization and follow-through

Stimulant Medications

What they are: Drugs like Ritalin and Concerta that increase dopamine and norepinephrine in the brain

Effect: Improve focus and motivation

In bipolar disorder: Can be used with caution and proper monitoring

Mood Stabilizer

What it is: Medications like ARISTADA that help prevent mood swings

Tapering

What it means: Gradually reducing a medication to avoid withdrawal or side effects

Superbill

What it is: An itemized form used for insurance reimbursement when seeing an out-of-network provider

🏠 Framework #2: Room-by-Room Organization

"Clean one room at a time. You don't go to another room and get caught up in that room."

The Four-Step Process

1 Survey Each Room and Categorize Items

Action: List items by use and location

2 In Week Two, Separate Items into Piles

Action: Based on category

3 (Optional) Place Items in Their Designated Spot

Action: Items that belong in the room go to their spot; leave items for other rooms in a pile to be moved later

4 Clean One Room at a Time

Critical rule: Avoid distraction by not moving to another room until finished

"Clean one room at a time. You don't go to another room and get caught up in that room."

Why this framework matters:

Breaking down cleaning and organization into manageable steps prevents overwhelm and increases the likelihood of follow-through, especially for individuals with ADHD

For you: You struggle with maintaining a clean home, often feeling overwhelmed. The therapist's stepwise, room-by-room approach is designed to match your executive functioning challenges, providing structure and reducing the risk of getting sidetracked.

Example: Instead of trying to clean the whole house at once (which leads to frustration and incomplete tasks), you will focus on one room, categorize, and separate items, making progress visible and manageable.

💊 Complete Medication Plan

ARISTADA (aripiprazole lauroxil) - MOOD STABILIZATION

Dosage: Every 4 weeks (exact dose to confirm with provider)

Purpose: Mood stabilization for Bipolar I Disorder

How to take: Administered by nurse at healthcare provider location

Expected benefits: Prevents manic and depressive episodes

Side effects to monitor: Sedation, weight gain, movement symptoms

Safety: Continue unless side effects or provider advises change

Rationale: Has prevented recent manic episodes; stable on current regimen

Wellbutrin (bupropion) - DISCONTINUE

Action: Discontinue immediately (no taper needed)

Previous purpose: For depression/ADHD, but not effective

Rationale: Making room for more effective ADHD treatment

Strattera (atomoxetine) - TAPER PLANNED

Action: Plan to taper (instructions to follow)

Previous purpose: ADHD, but not effective for you

Rationale: Replace with stimulant for better symptom control

Ritalin (methylphenidate) - NEW START

Dosage: 10mg twice daily (short-acting)

Start date: One week after stopping Wellbutrin

Purpose: Improve focus, motivation, and organization (ADHD)

How to take: First dose upon waking, second dose 4-6 hours later

Expected benefits: Increased energy, better attention

Side effects to monitor: Jitters, insomnia, appetite loss — monitor and report

Safety warnings: Do not exceed prescribed dose; avoid late-day dosing to prevent insomnia

Rationale: Evidence-based for ADHD, especially when mood is stabilized

How to take: After Ritalin, as directed
