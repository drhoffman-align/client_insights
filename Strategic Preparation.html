Therapeutic Insights

Alignment Psychiatry

The Situation

You are navigating multiple major transitions including an anticipated nonprofit promotion to full-time salaried work, leaving an unsustainable football job, starting a marketing role at a startup, and applying for NFL game day positions. You're managing housing and transportation logistics while preparing for an interview for a mentor position with at-risk youth.

Additionally, you're processing a recent breakup, establishing boundaries with an ex-partner, and adjusting to ongoing ADHD medication. Despite feeling productive and optimistic, you recognize stress levels and the need for strategic planning to avoid burnout.

Core Problem

You are balancing high-stakes career, financial, housing, and relationship transitions while maintaining momentum without burning out. There's anxiety about job security, pressure to prove worth in new roles, emotional residue from the breakup, and risk of overextending without prioritizing rest and sustainable self-care routines.

"Your genuine connection with youth is your greatest differentiator."

Clinical Concepts

Personal Brand
How you present your strengths, values, and unique qualities professionally—close to your identity but tailored for your audience.
Conflict Management
Handling disagreements to reach understanding and resolution rather than escalation.
Burnout
Emotional and physical exhaustion from prolonged stress, preventable through balanced activity and rest.
Medication Tolerance
Body adaptation to medication causing diminished effects over time.

Practical Instructions

Medication Plan:

Adderall 15mg once daily after breakfast. Take consistently to improve focus and executive functioning. May skip one day weekly to prevent tolerance. Monitor appetite suppression, dry mouth, GI discomfort if taken without food, and increased bathroom frequency. Maintain hydration and never take on empty stomach if causing discomfort.

Self-Care Reminder: Monitor for burnout signs including fatigue, irritability, and loss of motivation. Establish consistent routines for medication, sleep, and meals. Plan vacation or downtime before the school year starts.

Homework

  • Review job description and list all required qualities
  • Write brief stories demonstrating each quality from your experience
  • Practice telling stories aloud focusing on brevity and clarity
  • Prepare statement on "How will I make this position mine?"
  • Research organization's mission and incorporate their language
  • Schedule mock interview with colleague or friend
  • Complete before interview next week
