Therapeutic Insights

Alignment Psychiatry

The Situation

Rebecca is navigating significant grief while establishing a new life direction. She experiences guilt about her progress while friends remain in crisis, compounded by distressing world events she understands deeply through her security sector background. She struggles with chronic emotional overextension, habitually prioritizing others' needs while depleting her own resources.

Core Problem

"Rebecca exhibits an 'Atlas complex'—believing she must carry everyone's burdens to be worthy or helpful. This pattern causes emotional depletion, guilt about self-progress, and difficulty maintaining boundaries that protect her well-being."

Clinical Concepts

Grief
Emotional process of coping with loss
Atlas Complex
Belief one must carry others' burdens, leading to chronic over-responsibility and exhaustion
Emotional Bandwidth
Finite emotional energy available; overextension causes burnout
Holding Space
Being emotionally present without judgment

Practical Instructions

Understanding Your Emotional Bandwidth:

Your emotional energy is finite, like a battery that needs recharging. When you consistently prioritize others' needs over your own, you deplete this resource, leading to overwhelm and burnout. Recognizing where your energy flows—and ensuring you hold space for yourself first—is essential for sustainable well-being.

Setting Boundaries with Compassion:

Boundaries aren't walls that shut people out; they're guidelines that protect your well-being while maintaining meaningful connections. When you set a boundary, you're modeling self-respect and teaching others how to treat you. This isn't selfish—it's necessary for your capacity to show up authentically for yourself and others.

Breaking the Over-Giving Cycle:

The temporary relief you feel from helping others cannot sustain you when your own needs remain unmet. Notice when you're giving from an empty cup, and practice redirecting some of that care toward yourself. This shift allows you to support others from a place of fullness rather than depletion.

Homework

Daily Emotional Bandwidth Tracking (One Week):

  • Track your emotional bandwidth daily using a notebook or phone
  • Note energy spent on yourself versus others
  • Record moments of overwhelm as they occur
  • Identify when boundaries were set or needed
  • Reflect on one self-prioritization moment each day
  • Note one missed opportunity for boundary-setting
  • Bring your observations to the next session

Key Takeaways

  • Grief remains valid while moving forward
  • Guilt about progress doesn't indicate wrongdoing
  • Emotional bandwidth is finite; overextension guarantees overwhelm
  • Self-care enables sustainable support of others
  • Boundaries protect well-being and model self-respect
  • Awareness of patterns precedes change
  • The over-giving cycle can be broken
  • Prioritizing personal needs is necessary, not selfish
  • Temporary relief from helping others fails when own needs remain unmet
  • Adapting when plans change demonstrates self-compassion
Bottom Line:

You don't have to carry the weight of the world to be worthy or helpful. Your emotional bandwidth is finite, and protecting it through compassionate boundaries isn't selfish—it's essential. By holding space for yourself first, you create the foundation for sustainable care of others and genuine progress in your own life. The Atlas complex can be broken, one boundary at a time.
