Therapeutic Insights

Alignment Psychiatry

The Situation

You are navigating unemployment, irregular routines, and profound disillusionment with societal and political dysfunction. You repeatedly place hope in external systems and people to create change, only to face disappointment and rejection, including in your job search. This pattern leaves you depleted, stuck in survival mode, and neglecting your own needs while over-functioning for others who don't reciprocate.

"Waiting for external change is unproductive; focus on your control."

Core Problem

You rely on others—institutions, colleagues, society—to create the change you seek, leading to repeated disappointment and self-blame. This external focus, paired with poor boundaries and self-neglect, keeps you powerless and exhausted.

"Over-functioning for others creates depletion and resentment."

Clinical Concepts

External Locus of Control: Believing outcomes depend on others rather than your own actions.
Self-Blame: Taking excessive responsibility for things outside your control.
Survival Mode: Operating reactively rather than building stable routines.
Boundaries: Limits protecting your time and energy.
Reality Acceptance: Recognizing what is rather than wishing things were different.

Therapeutic Frameworks

Internal Agency and Self-Leadership

1
Recognize external reliance - Notice when you're waiting for others to create change or solve problems that affect your life.
2
Identify your own needs and resources - Clarify what you truly need and what skills and strengths you already possess.
3
Set boundaries and prioritize self-care - Protect your time and energy by establishing clear limits with others.
4
Take strategic action for yourself - Make deliberate choices that serve your goals and well-being, not just others' needs.

Key Insight: You possess skills to build your own path—use them. Your needs matter equally to others'—prioritize them.

Reality Acceptance vs. Fantasy Hope

1
Acknowledge reality that systems won't change on your timeline - Accept that external institutions and people operate independently of your hopes and needs.
2
Focus on what you can control - Direct your energy toward actions within your sphere of influence rather than wishing for different circumstances.
"Reality acceptance reduces frustration and wasted energy."

Practical Instructions

Building Foundation: Start with sleep routines as your foundational practice. Consistent sleep is essential for resilience, emotional regulation, and the energy needed to make other changes.

Identifying Needs: Take time to list your personal needs and desires independent of what others want from you or what you think should happen in the world. This is about you.

Setting Boundaries: Choose one specific boundary to implement this week. It might be saying no to a request, limiting time spent on draining activities, or protecting time for self-care.

Daily Actions: Each day, take at least one action that serves your goals and well-being, not just others' expectations or needs.

Remember: You're not responsible for fixing broken systems. Boundaries prevent burnout and protect your energy.

Homework

  • Establish consistent bedtime and wake time for two weeks
  • List personal needs and desires independent of others
  • Identify one boundary to set this week
  • Track sleep and boundary-setting experiences
