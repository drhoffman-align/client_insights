🗓️ Framework #1: Time Blocking for Life Management

"Time blocking is not just blocking off for current work, but blocking off basically your day."

The Five-Step Process

1 Identify Life Domains

What to do: List all areas needing regular attention

Your life domains:

  • Work
  • Cleaning/household tasks
  • Daughter time
  • Self-care
  • Personal business (YouTube content)
  • Sleep
  • Meals

Why this matters: Prevents neglecting important areas and reduces overwhelm by clarifying priorities

2 Assign Time Blocks

What to do: Allocate specific, recurring time slots for each domain

Examples for you:

  • 3-6pm Mondays: Work admin
  • Saturday mornings: Cleaning
  • Sunday afternoons: YouTube content creation
  • Wednesday 7-8pm: Daughter date
  • Daily 10-11pm: Me-time (consider moving to mornings)

Why this matters: Creates predictability and routine, making tasks feel more manageable

3 Treat Time Blocks as Non-Negotiable

The mindset shift: "Treat these blocks like executive meetings — they are sacred and not to be rescheduled."

What this means:

  • These appointments are with yourself
  • They're as important as any work meeting
  • Don't cancel or move them unless absolutely necessary

Why this matters: Increases follow-through and reduces temptation to procrastinate or multitask

4 Adjust for Realism

What to do: Start with manageable goals and adjust as needed to avoid overwhelm

Examples:

  • Shorter to-do lists: Limit to 3-5 items daily
  • Realistic cleaning schedules: One room per week vs. entire house
  • Smaller time blocks: 30 minutes instead of 2 hours

The therapist's guidance: "Start with manageable goals and adjust as needed to avoid overwhelm."

Why this matters: Prevents discouragement and shutdown when tasks feel insurmountable

5 Review and Refine

What to do: Regularly assess what's working and tweak the schedule or block sizes as needed

When to review: Weekly or biweekly

Why this matters: Ensures the system evolves with your needs and remains effective

📊 Before vs. After Time Blocking

Before: Overwhelmed by endless lists, paralyzed by missed tasks

After: Predictable routine, smaller achievable goals, less guilt for unfinished items

🌲 Framework #2: Forest View vs. Tree View

"You need a forest view, not a tree view. Zoom out and look at all the things you want to do."

The Two-Step Approach

1 Zoom Out (Forest View)

What this means: Periodically step back to see the big picture of your life and goals

What to ask yourself:

  • What are my overall goals for stability and well-being?
  • Am I neglecting any major life domain?
  • Are my daily efforts aligned with what truly matters?

Tools for maintaining forest view:

  • Wall calendars
  • Post-it notes
  • Vision boards

Why this matters: Prevents getting lost in details and helps prioritize what truly matters

2 Zoom In (Tree View)

What this means: Focus on specific tasks or problems only after clarifying their place in the bigger picture

Your tendency: You get bogged down in daily chaos ("tree view"), losing sight of broader goals

The balance: Zoom in to handle specific tasks, but only after you've zoomed out to see where they fit

Why this matters: Increases efficiency and reduces wasted effort on low-priority tasks

🛠️ Practical Instructions

Implement Time Blocking

  1. List all major life domains (work, cleaning, self, daughter, business, sleep, meals)
  2. Assign specific, recurring time blocks for each domain on weekly calendar
  3. Treat these blocks as non-negotiable appointments
  4. Start with small, realistic blocks and adjust as needed
  5. Use visual aids (wall calendar, post-its) to keep big picture in view

Refine To-Do Lists

  • Limit daily tasks to 3-5 items
  • Prioritize based on urgency and importance
  • Schedule breaks and "me time" intentionally

Sleep Hygiene Practices

To try:

  • Wind down with calming activities (journaling, fairy lights) instead of TV
  • Gradually shift "me time" to mornings if evening journaling disrupts sleep
  • Maintain consistent bedtime (aim for midnight or earlier)

The therapist's encouragement: "Keep trying — intentionally cut the TV off and do all the things for sleep hygiene."

Track Mood and Sleep

  • Use mood tracker to monitor emotional patterns
  • Note any changes after medication adjustments

Process Intrusive Thoughts

  • Write down persistent thoughts during "me time"
  • Reflect on their content and possible triggers
  • Avoid engaging with them late at night if it disrupts sleep

Support Your Daughter's Organization

Continue using tools like whiteboards and checklists to help her

Decompression Time After Work

Why this matters: Essential for mental health

Include in your time blocks: Transition time between work and other responsibilities

💊 Medication Plan

Vraylar (Cariprazine) - DOSAGE INCREASE

New dosage: Increase to 3mg (from current dose)

Important: Not all at once — titrate as directed

Timing: Once daily, as prescribed (at same time each day)

Purpose: Mood stabilization — reduce intensity of emotional highs and lows

How to take: With or without food

Expected benefits:

  • Smoother mood
  • Less emotional reactivity
  • Possible improvement in sleep

Important clarification: "It's not about eliminating emotion, but about blunting the highs and lows."

Side effects to monitor:

  • Drowsiness
  • Restlessness
  • Changes in appetite
  • Unusual movements
  • Worsening mood

Safety warnings: Report any severe side effects or sudden mood changes. Do not stop abruptly.

Rationale: Current dose not adequately controlling mood swings; higher dose may provide better stabilization

Temazepam - ONGOING

Timing: At bedtime as needed for sleep

Purpose: Aid in falling asleep

How to take: Shortly before bedtime

Expected benefits: Easier time falling asleep

Side effects to monitor: Next-day drowsiness, confusion, sleepwalking

Safety warnings: Use caution due to history of sleepwalking. Do not combine with alcohol or other sedatives.

Rationale: Helps initiate sleep, but does not maintain sleep

Xanax (Alprazolam) - EMERGENCIES ONLY

Dosage: 50 pills/month as needed

Purpose: Acute anxiety relief

How to take: As needed for severe anxiety or panic

Expected benefits: Rapid reduction in anxiety

Side effects to monitor: Drowsiness, dependence, confusion

Safety warnings: Use only as directed; risk of dependence

Rationale: For emergency use only, not for daily management
