💪 Reclaiming Power and Purpose

Learning to Put Yourself First

📍 The Situation

"If you're constantly working for everybody else, then how do you create stability for yourself?"

The Pattern

You consistently put others' needs before your own in professional and personal contexts. While sometimes rewarding, this longstanding pattern has resulted in emotional pain, lack of recognition, instability, and burnout. You generate ideas and effort, but others take credit or benefit more than you do. You've made others money and advanced their goals without receiving recognition or stability in return.

"Missing your feelings misses a lot of information."

Your tendency is to skip over your own hurt, focusing instead on helping others or moving to the next task. You're "multipurpose like Windex" — impressively versatile — but this versatility should benefit you, not just others.

The core problem: Chronic self-sacrifice and over-empathy leads to giving away your power without advocating for your own needs. Others benefit from your ideas without reciprocation, leaving you drained and exploited.

"Your empathy is outstanding. But it is — it's over the top."

⚡ The Power-Purpose Alignment Framework

"You should be able to actually control your purpose, your output, instead of letting others do that for you."

1 Notice Your Pattern

Recognize when you're prioritizing others at your own expense. Signs: offering ideas without ensuring credit, working hard for others' benefit with no return, excusing harmful behavior, skipping over your own pain to help someone else.

Why this matters: Noticing the pattern is the first step to change.

2 Process Your Feelings

Pause and ask: "What am I feeling right now?" Sit with any pain, disappointment, or frustration. Don't rush to fix or help someone else.

"Missing your feelings misses a lot of information."

Why this matters: Processing feelings prevents emotional buildup and provides valuable insight.

3 Extract Information

Use your feelings as data. Ask: What boundary was crossed? What did I need that I didn't get? What does this tell me about this relationship or situation?

Why this matters: Extracting information helps you learn from experience.

4 Strategic Adaptation

Apply what you've learned to future situations. Decide what you want to do differently: speak up for yourself, ask for recognition, say no, set clearer expectations upfront.

"Adaptability doesn't happen by ignoring things. Adaptability happens by taking notice."

Why this matters: Strategic adaptation increases resilience and prevents repeated exploitation.

5 Assert Ownership

Make sure your role is visible and acknowledged. You have the power to contribute ideas — now assert ownership instead of letting others claim your work.

Why this matters: Asserting ownership builds self-worth and credibility.

6 Align Actions with Purpose

Ensure your efforts serve both your own goals and, when appropriate, others' needs. Your purpose should serve you, not just others.

"Your purpose is just as strong as your power."

Why this matters: Aligning actions with purpose ensures fulfillment and sustainability.

✓ The Logic

Ignoring your own needs leads to burnout and being taken advantage of. By processing your emotions and learning from them, you can adapt, protect yourself, and ensure your efforts benefit you as well as others.


"The more power you have, the more you can do with that power."

📊 The Shift

Before: Help others, skip over own pain, end up feeling used or invisible.

After: Process feelings, learn from them, set boundaries so efforts are recognized and serve your own goals.

🔍 Key Concepts

Empathy

Understanding and sharing others' feelings. Your empathy is "over the top," leading to self-neglect. Empathy is a strength, but over-empathy can be self-destructive.

Boundaries

Limits you set to protect your well-being. Your lack of boundaries allows others to take advantage and leaves you exploited or overlooked.

Adaptability

Learning from experience and adjusting behavior. True adaptability requires processing emotions, not ignoring them.

"Adaptability doesn't happen by ignoring things. Adaptability happens by taking notice."

Accountability

Expecting others to take responsibility for their actions. Stop giving people "passes" because of their past.

"People actually do at some point have a choice. And so if they choose to be fucked up, it's their choice."

"Changing your behavior when it comes down to that — not giving passes — is very important."

🛠️ How to Apply This

The Six-Step Practice

  1. Notice when you're prioritizing others — pause and ask: "What am I feeling right now?"
  2. Sit with your feelings — don't rush to fix or help someone else
  3. Reflect on what your feelings tell you about your needs or boundaries
  4. Decide what to do differently next time (speak up, ask for recognition, say no)
  5. Ensure your contributions are visible and acknowledged
  6. Hold others accountable rather than excusing harmful behavior

What to Avoid

  • Skipping over your feelings
  • Giving away credit
  • Excusing harmful behavior
  • Working for everyone else without return

Remember: Not everyone deserves your energy or ideas — be selective.

📝 Your Assignment

Reflect on Your Purpose

Set aside 20-30 minutes in a quiet space. Write down:

  • Your core passions and abilities
  • What you want your work and life to look like
  • Recent situations where you gave away power or didn't get credit
  • For each: what you felt and what you wish you had done differently

Bring these notes to your next session.

Purpose: Clarify what truly matters to you and begin aligning actions with your own goals.

💪 The Bottom Line

This session highlighted the importance of reclaiming your power and aligning your actions with your own purpose, rather than continually sacrificing for others.

Your empathy and desire to help are strengths, but without boundaries and self-recognition, they become liabilities. By learning to process your feelings, assert ownership, and hold others accountable, you can break the cycle of self-neglect and exploitation.

The goal is not to stop helping others, but to ensure that your efforts also serve your own growth, stability, and fulfillment.

Moving forward, reflect deeply on your own purpose, passions, and needs. Use your emotional experiences as a guide for strategic adaptation. With these changes, you can expect to feel more empowered, recognized, and in control of your own narrative. 💪
