Procrastination

What it is: Delaying tasks, often due to low motivation or energy

What it can be linked to: Physical depletion or negative self-beliefs

Emotional Avoidance

What it is: Avoiding uncomfortable feelings or situations

How it shows up: Often by people-pleasing or not expressing true needs

People-Pleasing

What it is: Prioritizing others' approval over your own needs

The cost: Can lead to resentment and burnout

Kind vs. Nice

Belief-Emotion Link

💬 Framework #3: Nice vs. Kind Communication Model

"Communication is like buying a person a gift."

1 Recognize the Difference

"Nice": Surface-level and may be untruthful

"Kind": Involves honesty and care

"Kind tells the truth. Kind cares."

2 Communicate with Care

What to consider: The other person's needs and understanding

The metaphor: Communication is like buying a gift — you need to consider what the other person will appreciate, not just what you want to give

What to do: Before communicating, consider how the other person will receive your message

3 Practice Kindness

How: Tell the truth in a way that is considerate and supportive

For you: Practice being "kind" by telling the truth with care, rather than just being "nice"

Why this framework matters:

Authentic, kind communication builds trust and healthier relationships, reducing emotional avoidance

Your tendency toward people-pleasing and emotional avoidance can be addressed by practicing "kind" rather than just "nice" communication — being honest while still caring for others' feelings
