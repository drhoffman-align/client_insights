🌱 From Survival Mode to Self-Connection

Reclaiming Agency Amid Anxiety and Burnout

📍 The Situation: The Cost of Pushing Through

"Pushing forward seems like it is pushing further away from the need, which is connection to yourself."

What You're Experiencing

Current challenges:

  • Ongoing anxiety
  • OCD symptoms
  • Low mood
  • Work stress remains high
  • Feeling disconnected from yourself and your needs

What's not changing: Little improvement in symptoms despite attempts at self-care and engaging in hobbies

Your Background and Pattern

Your position: High-ranking position at work

What this has created: Pattern of overworking and "pushing through" difficulties

Your history: Anxiety, tried various medications

Your current state: "Survival mode" — little time or energy for genuine self-reflection or connection

The therapist's observation: "Your success here keeps you disconnected."

The Metaphors That Describe Where You Are

"Hamster wheel" — The relentless cycle of work and stress

"Graveyard" — The feeling of being stuck and depleted

"Thrown in the deep end" — Being overwhelmed by new work demands

The core problem: Chronic state of disconnection from self, driven by lifelong habit of powering through stress and external demands. This has led to emotional exhaustion, persistent anxiety, and lack of clarity about personal needs and desires.

The difficulty: Your success at work has come at the cost of self-neglect.

The mismatch: Outward achievements vs. internal sense of well-being.

The "push through" mentality: While effective for career advancement, now perpetuating burnout and emotional numbness.

"If something happens to you, they would replace you right away. It's business as usual."

Why Self-Care Hasn't Worked

What happens: You feel lost when not working, unsure what you like or need

The result: Attempts at self-care feel hollow because they're not rooted in self-knowledge

The therapist's insight: "Self-care involves a person knowing what they need in self-care."

🔄 Framework #1: Connection vs. Survival Mode

"You can't direct the world on how to treat you if you don't know how to treat yourself."

The Five-Step Process

1 Recognize Survival Mode

What to notice: When you're operating on autopilot, just getting through each day

What survival mode is: Operating on autopilot, focused only on getting through the day, often at the expense of emotional and physical health

Why this matters: Survival mode keeps you stuck in stress and prevents healing

2 Identify Disconnection

What to acknowledge: Feelings of numbness, exhaustion, or lack of motivation as signs of being disconnected from yourself

Signs you're disconnected:

  • Numbness
  • Exhaustion
  • Lack of motivation
  • Unsure what you like or need
  • Self-care feels hollow

What self-connection is: Being attuned to your own feelings, needs, and desires. It's the opposite of numbness or self-neglect.

3 Shift Focus to Self-Connection

What to do: Instead of pushing harder, pause and ask, "What do I need right now?"

The shift: From external demands to internal awareness

Why this matters: Self-connection is the foundation for sustainable well-being and authentic decision-making

4 Practice Gentle Self-Care

What to do: Engage in activities that are nurturing, not just distracting or obligatory

What true self-care is: Activities that nurture and restore you. True self-care requires knowing what you actually need, not just following generic advice.

The therapist's guidance: "Connecting to yourself is being gentle and graceful with yourself, not being beholden to someone else over you and what you need."

5 Reorganize Priorities

What to do: Place your own needs and well-being at the center, rather than external expectations

The shift needed: From "what others need from me" to "what I need for myself"

The reminder: "You have to be the one to protect you, because nobody else will — especially not like you."

✓ The Logic Behind This Framework

When you're disconnected, you're running on empty and can't direct others (or the world) on how to treat you.

True change starts with reconnecting to your own needs and feelings.


"You can't direct the world on how to treat you if you don't know how to treat yourself."

📊 Before vs. After

Before: Life is dictated by external demands, leading to exhaustion and anxiety

After: With time and self-connection, you can make choices based on your own needs, leading to greater well-being

🌅 Framework #2: Exposure to Self-Connection (Breaks from Work)

"It's beyond taking a break. It's beyond sleep. It's about literally waking up and not knowing what to do — and being safe in that."

The Four-Step Process

1 Consider Structured Breaks

Options available:

  • Full leave (FMLA/short-term disability)
  • Regular days off

What FMLA/Short-Term Disability is: Legal protections that allow for extended time off work for health reasons, providing space for recovery and self-exploration

Privacy consideration: Contact HR to understand the process and privacy protections — who will have access to your health information?

2 Allow for Adjustment

What to expect: First weeks to feel strange, with tiredness and anxiety as you "get off the hamster wheel"

The therapist's guidance: "Getting off the hamster wheel takes about a month to actually calm down."

The metaphor: Like getting off a treadmill that's been running at full speed — your body and mind need time to adjust to stillness

Why this discomfort is normal: You've been in survival mode for so long that rest and self-connection will initially feel foreign

3 Explore Freedom

What to do: Use the time to experiment with unstructured activities, noticing what you enjoy or dislike

What exposure therapy means (in this context): Gradually exposing yourself to the discomfort of not working or being productive, in order to learn how to be with yourself

The goal: Rediscover who you are outside of constant productivity

"It's about literally waking up and not knowing what to do — and being safe in that."

4 Rebuild Routine

What to do: Gradually create a life structure based on your own preferences, not just external demands

The shift: From routines imposed by work to routines that serve your well-being

What might emerge: "Seriously consider trying to connect. A lot of things can come out of it — even you finding a new job."

✓ Why This Framework Matters

Time away from work is not just about rest — it's about rediscovering who you are outside of constant productivity

Without a break, the cycle of stress and disconnection continues

Structured time off allows for the possibility of genuine self-discovery and healing

🛠️ Practical Instructions

What to Do

  1. Reflect on Your Needs: Regularly pause and ask yourself what you truly need, beyond just "pushing through"
  2. Consider Taking a Structured Break: Explore options for FMLA or regular days off. Contact HR to understand the process and privacy protections
  3. Monitor Medication Changes: Follow the tapering schedule for lamotrigine as outlined, and track any withdrawal symptoms
  4. Experiment with Self-Connection: Use time off to try new activities, notice your reactions, and begin to rebuild a sense of self
  5. Communicate with Your Therapist: Use the Simple Practice app to report on medication effects and emotional changes

When to Use Each Strategy

Daily: Use self-reflection and gentle self-care

As soon as feasible: Initiate structured breaks

As scheduled: Apply medication changes

Anytime needed: Reach out to your therapist if symptoms worsen or questions arise

What to Avoid

  • Defaulting to "pushing through" without checking in with yourself
  • Ignoring withdrawal symptoms or emotional shifts during medication changes
  • Making decisions solely based on external expectations

💊 Medication Plan

Lamotrigine (Lamictal) - TAPER SCHEDULE

Current dose: 100mg (capsule, can be split if scored)

Taper schedule: Decrease by 25mg every two weeks

Example: 100mg → 75mg → 50mg → 25mg → stop

How to take: Split tablets as needed; supplement with 25mg tablets as prescribed

Purpose: Used for mood stabilization; being tapered due to side effects and interaction concerns

Expected benefits: Reduce side effects, allow for introduction of new medication

Withdrawal symptoms to monitor:

  • Tiredness
  • Drowsiness
  • Irritability
  • "Brain zaps" (mild electrical sensations in the head)

Safety warnings: Report any severe mood swings, irritability, or unusual symptoms

Rationale: Lamotrigine has many side effects and interaction issues; a slow taper minimizes withdrawal and prepares for possible new medication

What medication tapering is: Gradually reducing medication dosage to minimize withdrawal effects and monitor for changes in mood or anxiety

Clonazepam (Klonopin) - ANXIOLYTIC

Dose: 2mg, trialing half or quarter doses as needed

Purpose: Anxiolytic (reduces anxiety), used for acute symptom relief

How to take: Try half a dose on weekends; adjust as tolerated

Expected benefits: Reduce anxiety and grogginess, help with sleep

Side effects to monitor: Grogginess, dependency risk, drowsiness

Safety warnings: Use only as directed; risk of dependence with long-term use

Rationale: Chosen for longer-acting anxiety relief compared to Xanax; trialing lowest effective dose

Iron Supplements

Purpose: Address low iron, which can worsen anxiety and fatigue

How to take: As prescribed; continue monitoring iron levels

Expected benefits: Improved energy, reduced anxiety

Side effects to monitor: GI upset, constipation

Safety warnings: Ensure adequate dosing; follow up with labs as needed

Future Medication Considerations

Fluvoxamine: Not started due to interaction concerns with current regimen

Lorazepam: May be considered after lamotrigine taper if needed for anxiety
