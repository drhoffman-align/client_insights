  • Unexpected changes to lease

"Skipping steps creates more problems. You start building a business around putting out fires instead of building something sustainable."

🏗️ The Foundation-Building Framework for Stability

"Waking up earlier gives you peace before the day starts — time to think about what you want and need."

The Five-Step Process

1 Time and Energy Optimization

What to do: Block out time for personal development, business planning, and self-care

When to do it: Prioritize these blocks at the start of the day, before family and work demands

Specific action: Wake up 1-1.5 hours before your kids wake up. Use this time for personal development and planning.

Why this matters: Early focus allows for clear thinking before anxiety and external pressures set in

How this applies to you: Your mornings are often reactive, filled with family and work demands. By waking earlier and dedicating time to planning and reflection, you can approach your day with intention rather than anxiety.

2 Sleep-Wake Cycle Reset

What to do: Plan a consistent bedtime and wake-up time

Support strategy: Use medication as a temporary aid to establish this routine

What sleep hygiene is: Practices that support consistent, restful sleep — includes regular bedtime, winding down routines, and using medication as needed

Why this matters: Consistent sleep improves energy, mood, and cognitive function

How this applies to you: Your current pattern of late nights and restless sleep perpetuates your fatigue and anxiety. Using medication to establish a new routine will help you feel more rested and capable.

"Consistency is key. It takes about three months to solidify a new habit."

3 Written Processing

What to do: Handwrite plans, brainstorms, and reflections during morning personal time

Tools: Use pen and paper, or stylus and tablet

Why this matters: Writing by hand solidifies thoughts and helps connect ideas more deeply

"Handwriting your plans helps solidify your thoughts — writing connects ideas in a way typing doesn't."

How this applies to you: Typing or thinking about plans hasn't been effective for you. Handwriting your thoughts will help you clarify your goals and next steps.

4 SMART Goal Setting

What SMART means:

  • Specific
  • Measurable
  • Achievable
  • Relevant
  • Time-bound

What to do: Visualize what success looks like in concrete terms

"SMART goals aren't just about what you want — they're about picturing exactly what success looks like."

Why this matters: Clear goals provide direction and reduce overwhelm

How this applies to you: You want to double your revenue but lack a clear picture of how to achieve this. By defining specific, actionable steps, you can move from vague aspirations to concrete progress.

5 Legal and Documentation Strategy

What to do: Document all agreements and unmet expectations with landlord

How: Create a paper trail via email for legal protection

What a paper trail is: Written documentation of agreements and communications, crucial for legal protection in disputes

"You need to create a paper trail. If it's not in writing, it won't hold up in court."

"If you don't document your agreements, you lose your leverage."

Why this matters: Written records are essential for legal recourse and clarity

How this applies to you: Your verbal agreements with landlord have led to unmet expectations and financial risk. Creating a written record will strengthen your position and provide options beyond bankruptcy.

🔍 Understanding Key Concepts

Anxiety

What it is: A heightened state of worry or fear, often triggered by perceived threats to safety or stability

In your case: Anxiety is both a cause and consequence of your unstable work and living situation

How it affects you: Poor sleep worsens anxiety and impairs logical thinking

Fight or Flight Response

What it is: The body's automatic reaction to stress, which can lead to impulsive decisions and short-term problem-solving

In your case: Your anxiety often pushes you into this mode, making it hard to plan for the long term

"When anxiety is up, you're not able to think logically. Survival mode makes you act fast, but not always smart."

🛠️ Practical Instructions

1. Wake Up Earlier

Set your alarm for 1-1.5 hours before your kids wake up. Use this time for personal development and planning.

2. Take Medication Immediately

Upon waking, take your Adderall and Vyvanse to ensure you're alert and focused during your morning routine.

3. Handwrite Your Plans

Use pen and paper (or stylus and tablet) to brainstorm, set goals, and reflect on your business and life.

4. Establish a Consistent Sleep Schedule

Choose a bedtime that allows for adequate rest. Use prescribed sleep medication as needed to help fall and stay asleep.

5. Block Your Time

Schedule specific periods for work, family, fitness, and personal reflection. Stick to these blocks as consistently as possible.

"It's okay to dedicate more time to work now if it means gaining stability faster."

6. Document Landlord Interactions

Walk through your leased space and list all unmet expectations and changes. Write down all relevant conversations, dates, and agreements. Begin emailing your landlord to create a written record.

7. Set SMART Goals

Define what doubling your revenue looks like, what steps are required, and how you'll measure progress.

8. Seek Legal Advice

Once documentation is complete, consult with a legal professional — consider friends, family, or community resources if cost is an issue.

"Legal help doesn't always mean expensive — think about friends, family, or community resources."

What to Avoid

  • Skipping steps in your routine or documentation process
  • Relying solely on memory or verbal agreements
  • Letting anxiety dictate your daily actions

Assignment #2: Create SMART Goals for Your Business

You don't need to do all of these steps in one sitting. Do 1 part of this assignment daily.

Process:

  1. Define your goal: What is the goal if resources aren't an issue?
  2. Determine what achievement looks like: When you have a goal, determine what it will look like in business and in life when this goal is achieved
  3. Visualize your successful week: Imagine you've achieved your goal — what does it look like as you're walking through your work week? Think of every day — Monday through Friday
  4. Go through each part of your business: Imagine how it functions when the goal is met

Purpose: To clarify your goals and establish a foundation for stability

💊 Medication Plan

Adderall (5mg) and Vyvanse - MORNING STIMULANTS

Dose: Adderall 5mg (half of a 10mg tablet) and Vyvanse (current dose)

Timing: Take immediately upon waking

Purpose: To increase motivation and energy throughout the day

Expected benefits: Improved focus, motivation, and ability to engage in personal development and business planning

Side effects to monitor: Insomnia, jitteriness, appetite changes, increased anxiety

Safety warnings: Do not take more than prescribed; monitor for increased anxiety or sleep disruption

Rationale: Using stimulants in the morning helps you be productive and focused during your most important planning time

Sleep Medication (To Be Started)

Purpose: To help fall asleep and stay asleep, improving overall rest and energy

How to take: Take as prescribed, ideally 30-60 minutes before planned bedtime

Expected benefits: Easier time falling asleep, more restful sleep, less daytime fatigue

Side effects to monitor: Drowsiness, grogginess, potential dependency

Safety warnings: Use only as directed; avoid combining with alcohol or other sedatives

Rationale: Short-term use to help reset sleep-wake cycle and establish a consistent routine. Use medication strategically to support new routines, not as a permanent solution.
