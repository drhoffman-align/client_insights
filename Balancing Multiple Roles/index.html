⏰ Framework: Time Blocking vs. Time Block Scheduling

"Time blocking typically works a little bit better, or a time block schedule works a little bit better because you can kinda do whatever you wanna do in there."

Understanding the Difference

Time Blocking (Flexible Approach)

What it is: Block out periods for specific activities (work, business, personal time)

How it works:

  • Flexible within the block — do what feels right in that window
  • Focus on one domain during that block
  • Adjust as needed day-to-day

Benefits:

  • Reduces decision fatigue
  • Helps focus
  • Allows responsiveness to energy levels

Time Block Schedule (Consistent Approach)

What it is: Assigns consistent blocks for recurring activities

Examples:

  • Every morning: Personal development
  • Afternoons: Work
  • Evenings: Business

Benefits:

  • Builds habits and predictability
  • Allows for distribution of tasks over the week
  • Creates rhythm and routine

The therapist's guidance: "Stick to a schedule type of thing — but with flexibility."

How You're Using This

Personal care (morning): Consistent time block for self-care routines

Work: Dedicated daytime blocks

Business development: Lunch breaks, evenings, and weekends

✓ Why This Framework Matters

Prevents overwhelm by creating structure without rigidity

Helps prioritize and reduces distractions

Supports habit formation

By having dedicated time for each domain, you can be present and effective rather than scattered or reactive

😴 Sleep Consistency and Energy Management

"Don't worry about sleep because sleep is something that, you know, my body's gonna just do."

Wake Up at the Same Time Daily

The principle: Consistent wake time matters more than consistent bedtime

Why this works:

  • Reinforces the body's internal clock (circadian rhythm)
  • Reduces anxiety about sleep
  • Promotes stable energy throughout the day

Your target: At least six hours of sleep, adjusting wake times based on bedtime

Don't Stress About Sleep Duration

The mindset shift: Focus on consistent wake time; the body will adjust

How this works:

  • If tired, sleep will naturally come earlier the next night
  • Your body self-regulates when wake time is consistent
  • Worrying about sleep often makes it worse

The therapist's reassurance: "Don't worry about sleep because sleep is something that, you know, my body's gonna just do."

✓ Why This Matters

Consistency in sleep and wake times stabilizes mood and energy

Makes it easier to maintain routines and productivity

Reduces sleep anxiety
